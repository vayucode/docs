## Overview

Use the mark as paid feature to manually update an invoice's payment status when you receive payment through channels not integrated with Vayu, such as wire transfers, checks, or external payment processors.

## When to mark invoices as paid

Mark invoices as paid manually when:

- **Wire transfers** - Customer pays via bank transfer outside of Stripe or other integrated payment systems
- **Check payments** - Customer sends a physical check
- **External payment processors** - Payment is processed through a system not connected to Vayu
- **Cash payments** - Customer pays in cash (less common for B2B)
- **Payment reconciliation** - Correcting payment status after manual verification

## Marking an invoice as paid

### Through the UI

1. Navigate to **Invoices**
2. Locate the invoice you want to mark as paid
3. Click on the invoice to open the detail view
4. Click **Mark as Paid**
5. Confirm the action

The invoice status will update to **Paid** and the paid date will be recorded.

### Through the API

Use the payment status endpoint to retrieve and verify invoice payment information:

## Custom field columns

You can surface **customer** and **contract** custom field values as optional columns on the **Payments** page, making it easier to slice, sort, and reconcile payments using the same custom fields you already track on customers and contracts.

### When to use custom field columns

Add custom field columns when you need to:

- Group payments by attributes that are not part of the default table (for example, region, segment, billing entity, or contract type).
- Reconcile payments against external systems that key off your custom field values.
- Export a payments view that includes the metadata your finance or RevOps team uses elsewhere in Vayu.

### Showing or hiding columns

1. Navigate to **Payments**.
2. Open the column picker above the table.
3. Toggle on any **customer** or **contract** custom field you want to display.

Your column selection is saved automatically and persists the next time you open the page.

<Note>
  Payments that are not linked to an invoice (such as standalone payments or overpayments) have no associated contract, so contract custom field columns are empty for those rows. Customer custom fields still appear as long as the payment is linked to a customer.
</Note>

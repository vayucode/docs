Spend thresholds allow you to set maximum spending limits on usage-based products to control costs and prevent unexpected charges. When a customer reaches their configured threshold, you can automatically stop further usage or trigger notifications.

## Overview

Use spend thresholds to:

- **Prevent budget overruns** – Cap customer spending at a predefined amount
- **Control costs** – Ensure customers don't exceed their allocated budget
- **Trigger alerts** – Notify customers or internal teams when thresholds are approached or reached
- **Enforce limits** – Automatically stop service when spending limits are hit

Spend thresholds are particularly useful for usage-based pricing models where consumption can vary significantly period-to-period.

---

## How spend thresholds work

Spend thresholds monitor accumulated charges for usage-based products during a billing period. When the total spend reaches the configured threshold amount:

1. **Soft threshold** – Triggers a notification but allows continued usage
2. **Hard threshold** – Stops further usage until the next billing period or manual override

You can configure thresholds at different levels:

- **Product level** – Apply limits to individual metered products
- **Plan level** – Set overall spending caps across all products in a plan
- **Customer level** – Define custom limits for specific customers

---

## Configuring spend thresholds

To set up a spend threshold:

1. Navigate to **Plans & Pricing** → Select your plan → **Add pricing & products**
2. Select the usage-based product you want to limit
3. Click **Modify** → **+ Set Spend Threshold**
4. Configure the threshold settings:
   - **Threshold amount** – Maximum spend allowed (in USD)
   - **Threshold type** – Soft (notify only) or Hard (block usage)
   - **Reset period** – When the threshold counter resets (monthly, quarterly, annually)
   - **Notification recipients** – Who receives alerts when threshold is reached

5. Click **Save** to apply the threshold

<Tip>
  **Best practices** <br/>
  Set soft thresholds at 80-90% of your hard limit to give customers advance warning. <br/>
  Configure multiple notification recipients to ensure alerts are not missed. <br/>
  Align reset periods with your billing intervals for consistency.
</Tip>

## Threshold types

### Soft thresholds

Soft thresholds trigger notifications when reached but do not block usage. Use soft thresholds to:

- Warn customers they are approaching their limit
- Alert internal teams to review customer usage patterns
- Provide early visibility into potential overages

Customers can continue using the service after a soft threshold is reached.

### Hard thresholds

Hard thresholds automatically stop further usage when the spending limit is reached. Use hard thresholds to:

- Enforce strict budget controls
- Prevent unexpected charges
- Comply with customer spending agreements

When a hard threshold is hit, the service is suspended until:
- The next reset period begins
- An administrator manually increases the limit
- The customer upgrades to a higher plan

## Threshold notifications

Configure notifications to alert stakeholders when thresholds are reached:

**Notification triggers:**
- Threshold reached (100%)
- Threshold warning (configurable percentage, e.g., 80%, 90%)
- Threshold exceeded (for soft thresholds only)

**Notification channels:**
- Email to specified recipients
- Webhook events for programmatic handling
- In-app notifications in the customer portal

## Monitoring and reporting

Track threshold performance through:

- **Usage dashboard** – Real-time view of current spend vs. threshold limits
- **Threshold alerts** – Historical log of all threshold events
- **Customer reports** – Per-customer threshold utilization and breach history

Use these insights to:
- Identify customers who frequently hit limits
- Adjust thresholds based on usage patterns
- Optimize pricing tiers and plans

---

## Threshold resets

Thresholds automatically reset based on the configured reset period:

- **Monthly** – Resets on the first day of each calendar month
- **Quarterly** – Resets every three months from contract start date
- **Annually** – Resets once per year from contract start date
- **Billing period** – Resets according to the customer's billing cycle

After a reset, the spend counter returns to zero and usage resumes normally.

<Note>
  Threshold resets are independent of billing intervals. You can meter usage monthly but apply quarterly spending thresholds, or vice versa.
</Note>

## Use cases

### Freemium plans

Set hard thresholds to enforce free tier limits:
- Allow $10 of free usage per month
- Block usage when threshold is reached
- Prompt customers to upgrade to paid plans

### Enterprise cost controls

Use soft thresholds for enterprise customers:
- Set warning at 80% of agreed budget
- Notify account managers to discuss usage
- Prevent surprise invoices at month-end

### Trial periods

Combine thresholds with time-based trials:
- Offer $100 of free usage during 30-day trial
- Hard threshold prevents abuse
- Automatically convert to paid plan after trial
